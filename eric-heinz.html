Interview with Eric Heinz

Interviewer
Eric, your work explores the intersection of sound and visual art. How do you approach creating these multimedia experiences?
Eric Heinz
Sound and image are inseparable for me. I think of them as different frequencies of the same energy. When I'm creating, I'm not thinking about adding sound to visuals or visuals to sound—I'm thinking about how to make the whole experience feel like one unified organism.
Interviewer
Your installations often respond to environmental data in real-time. What interests you about this approach?
Eric Heinz
I'm fascinated by the idea that art can be alive, that it can breathe and change and respond to the world around it. When you walk into one of my installations, you're not just experiencing my vision—you're experiencing the space itself, the air, the light, the energy of everyone else who's there.
Interviewer
How do you balance technical complexity with artistic expression?
Eric Heinz
The technology should be invisible. If people are thinking about the code or the sensors or the algorithms, then I've failed. The technology is just the medium—like paint or clay—and the art is what emerges from that medium. I want people to feel something, not to think about how it was made.
