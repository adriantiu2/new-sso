Interview with Ingrid Lu

Interviewer
Ingrid, your work explores the intersection of data visualization and emotional expression. How do you approach making abstract data feel personal and meaningful?
Ingrid Lu
Data is just information, but information becomes meaningful when it connects to human experience. I'm interested in finding the emotional core of quantitative information—how do you make a spreadsheet feel like a love letter, or a graph feel like a memory?
Interviewer
Your recent project visualized the emotional patterns of social media users. What did you discover about the relationship between data and feeling?
Ingrid Lu
The most interesting thing was how predictable our emotions can be, but also how unique each person's emotional fingerprint is. We all experience joy and sadness, but the patterns, the rhythms, the ways we move through those feelings—that's what makes us human. Data can reveal that humanity.
Interviewer
What's the future of data art? How do you see it evolving?
Ingrid Lu
I think we're moving toward more intimate, more personal data experiences. Instead of big, abstract visualizations, we'll see art that responds to your heartbeat, your breathing, your mood. The goal isn't to make data beautiful—it's to make it feel alive, to make it feel like it understands you.
