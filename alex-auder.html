Interview with Alex auder

Interviewer
Alex, your work has been described as pushing the boundaries of digital art. What draws you to explore the intersection of technology and creativity?
Alex Auder
I've always been fascinated by the way technology can amplify human expression. There's something magical about taking something intangible—an emotion, a memory, a concept—and giving it form through code and pixels. It's like being a digital alchemist.
Interviewer
Your recent installation used AI to generate real-time responses to viewer emotions. How do you see artificial intelligence changing the art world?
Alex Auder
AI isn't replacing human creativity—it's expanding it. Think of it as a collaborator that can process patterns and possibilities at a scale humans can't. The real art happens in how we direct and interpret what emerges from that collaboration.
Interviewer
What advice would you give to emerging artists who want to work with technology?
Alex Auder
Start with the concept, not the technology. The tools are just means to an end. Focus on what you want to say or explore, then find the right medium—whether that's code, VR, or something that doesn't exist yet. And don't be afraid to break things. Some of my best work came from happy accidents.
