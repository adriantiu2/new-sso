Interview with Princess Demeny

Interviewer
Princess, your work often challenges traditional notions of femininity and power. Can you tell us about your approach to these themes?
Princess Demeny
I'm interested in reclaiming and redefining what it means to be powerful as a woman. There's this idea that femininity and strength are somehow opposed, but I think that's a false binary. My work explores how we can be both soft and fierce, vulnerable and commanding.
Interviewer
Your use of bold, confrontational imagery has been both celebrated and controversial. How do you navigate that response?
Princess Demeny
Controversy often means you're touching a nerve, and that's usually where the most important conversations happen. I'm not trying to shock for shock's sake—I'm trying to start conversations about things that matter. If my work makes people uncomfortable, that might be exactly what they need to feel.
Interviewer
What's your creative process like? How do you decide what to explore next?
Princess Demeny
I follow my anger, my curiosity, and my joy. If something makes me feel strongly—whether that's rage at injustice or delight in something beautiful—that's usually a sign I should explore it. Art is how I process the world and try to make sense of my place in it.
