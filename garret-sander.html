Interview with Garret Sander

Interviewer
Garret, your work often explores the relationship between physical and digital spaces. How do you approach creating these hybrid environments?
Garret Sander
I'm interested in the idea that we're living in multiple realities simultaneously. There's the physical world we can touch and see, and then there are all these digital layers that exist on top of it. My work is about making those invisible layers visible, about showing people that the digital and physical aren't separate—they're intertwined.
Interviewer
Your installations often respond to environmental data in real-time. What interests you about this approach?
Garret Sander
I love the idea that art can be alive, that it can breathe and change and respond to the world around it. When you walk into one of my installations, you're not just experiencing my vision—you're experiencing the space itself, the air, the light, the energy of everyone else who's there. It's about making the environment itself part of the artwork.
Interviewer
What's the future of interactive art? How do you see it evolving?
Garret Sander
I think we're moving toward art that's not just interactive, but truly responsive. Instead of just reacting to what you do, future art will anticipate what you need, what you're feeling, what you're thinking. It's about creating experiences that feel like they understand you, that feel like they're alive and aware.
