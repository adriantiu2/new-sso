Interview with Lexi Langil

Interviewer
Lexi, your work often explores themes of transformation and metamorphosis. What draws you to these concepts?
Lexi Langil
I'm fascinated by the idea that we're all constantly changing, constantly becoming something new. There's something beautiful and terrifying about that process—the way we shed old versions of ourselves and grow into new ones. My work is about capturing those moments of transition, those in-between states.
Interviewer
Your recent series used augmented reality to show viewers different versions of themselves. What was the thinking behind this approach?
Lexi Langil
I wanted to create an experience where people could see themselves as they might be, as they could be, as they already are in some parallel universe. It's about expanding our sense of possibility, showing people that they're not fixed, that they have the power to transform themselves.
Interviewer
How do you see technology changing our relationship with identity?
Lexi Langil
Technology is giving us new tools for self-expression and self-exploration. We can create avatars, filter our images, present different versions of ourselves online. But I think the real question is: how do we use these tools to become more authentic, more fully ourselves, rather than just creating more masks to hide behind?
