Interview with Melanie Wu

Interviewer
Melanie, your work often incorporates elements of traditional Chinese art and contemporary digital media. How do you navigate between these different cultural and temporal contexts?
Melanie Wu
I don't see them as separate—they're all part of the same continuum of human expression. Traditional Chinese painting has always been about capturing the essence of a moment, the spirit of a place. Digital media just gives us new tools to explore those same fundamental questions about beauty, time, and meaning.
Interviewer
Your use of calligraphy in digital spaces is particularly striking. What draws you to this combination?
Melanie Wu
Calligraphy is about the gesture, the movement of the hand, the breath. When I translate that into digital space, I'm not just copying the form—I'm trying to capture that same sense of flow, that same connection between body and spirit. It's about making the digital feel human.
Interviewer
What role do you see tradition playing in contemporary art?
Melanie Wu
Tradition isn't about preserving the past—it's about understanding the present. When I work with traditional forms, I'm not trying to recreate something that's already been done. I'm trying to find new ways to express timeless human experiences using the tools and language of our time.
