Interview with Liana Satenstein

Interviewer
Liana, your work often explores the intersection of fashion and digital art. How do you see these two worlds coming together?
Liana Satenstein
Fashion has always been about identity and self-expression, and digital art is just another tool for exploring those same themes. I'm interested in how we can use technology to create clothing and accessories that respond to our emotions, our environment, our mood. It's about making fashion feel alive.
Interviewer
Your recent collection featured garments that change color based on the wearer's biometric data. What inspired this approach?
Liana Satenstein
I wanted to create clothing that's truly personal, that reflects not just how you want to look, but how you actually feel. When your shirt changes color with your heartbeat, or your dress responds to your stress levels, it becomes a kind of emotional mirror. It's about making the invisible visible.
Interviewer
What's the future of wearable technology in fashion?
Liana Satenstein
I think we're moving toward clothing that's not just smart, but empathetic. Instead of just tracking your steps or monitoring your heart rate, future fashion will understand your emotional state and respond accordingly. It's about creating a deeper connection between what we wear and who we are.
