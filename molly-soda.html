Interview with Molly soda

Interviewer
Molly, your work often explores themes of identity and self-expression in digital spaces. What inspired you to focus on these particular themes?
Molly Soda
I grew up online, so I've always been interested in how we construct and perform identity in digital spaces. There's this fascinating tension between authenticity and performance—how much of our online selves is "real" and how much is curated? I want to explore that gray area.
Interviewer
Your use of bright, saturated colors and playful imagery creates a very distinctive aesthetic. How did you develop this visual language?
Molly Soda
I was really influenced by early internet aesthetics—the garish colors, the pixelated graphics, the way everything felt both artificial and deeply personal. I wanted to capture that feeling of being overwhelmed by digital stimuli while also finding beauty in the chaos.
Interviewer
What role do you think social media plays in contemporary art and culture?
Molly Soda
Social media has democratized art in some ways—anyone can share their work and find an audience. But it's also created new pressures around visibility and validation. I'm interested in how artists navigate that landscape, how we maintain creative integrity while engaging with these platforms.
