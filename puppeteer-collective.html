Interview with Puppeteer collective

Interviewer
Puppeteer Collective, your work often explores themes of control and agency through digital puppetry. What draws you to this particular medium?
Puppeteer Collective
Puppetry is such a powerful metaphor for the human condition. We're all puppets in some way—controlled by our desires, our fears, our circumstances. But we're also puppeteers, pulling the strings of our own lives and the lives of others. Digital puppetry just gives us new tools to explore that dynamic.
Interviewer
Your recent performance involved audience members controlling digital puppets in real-time. What was that experience like?
Puppeteer Collective
It was fascinating to watch people discover their own power and responsibility. When you give someone control over a digital puppet, they have to make choices about how to move it, what to make it do. Some people were gentle and careful, others were aggressive and chaotic. It revealed a lot about human nature.
Interviewer
What role do you see technology playing in the future of performance art?
Puppeteer Collective
Technology is giving us new ways to tell stories, new ways to create characters, new ways to connect with audiences. But the fundamental questions remain the same: What does it mean to be human? What does it mean to have agency? What does it mean to be in control? Technology just gives us new tools to explore those timeless questions.
