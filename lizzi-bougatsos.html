Interview with Lizzi Bougatsos

Interviewer
Lizzi, your work spans multiple mediums—music, visual art, performance. How do you approach these different forms of expression?
Lizzi Bougatsos
I don't really think of them as separate things. Sound has color, color has rhythm, movement has melody. When I'm creating, I'm just trying to capture whatever energy is moving through me at that moment, whether that comes out as a song, a painting, or a performance.
Interviewer
Your performances often involve found objects and unconventional materials. What draws you to this approach?
Lizzi Bougatsos
I love the idea that everything has potential, that any object can become an instrument or a canvas. There's something magical about taking something that's been discarded or overlooked and giving it new life, new meaning. It's about finding the extraordinary in the ordinary.
Interviewer
How do you see the relationship between art and everyday life?
Lizzi Bougatsos
Art isn't something that happens in a gallery or a studio—it's something that happens in the kitchen, on the street, in the shower. I want to make work that reminds people that creativity is everywhere, that we're all artists, that every moment has the potential to be beautiful and meaningful.
