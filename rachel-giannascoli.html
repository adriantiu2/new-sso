Interview with Rachel Giannascoli

Interviewer
Rachel, your work often explores themes of intimacy and vulnerability through digital media. How do you approach these deeply personal subjects?
Rachel Giannascoli
I think vulnerability is one of the most powerful things we can share. In a world where everyone is trying to present a perfect version of themselves, there's something radical about being honest about our flaws, our fears, our messy humanity. Digital media just gives us new ways to explore that honesty.
Interviewer
Your recent project involved live-streaming your creative process. What was that experience like?
Rachel Giannascoli
It was terrifying and liberating at the same time. Usually, I get to edit and curate what people see, but with live-streaming, there's no hiding. People saw me struggle, make mistakes, get frustrated. But that's also where the real connection happens—when people see that you're human, just like them.
Interviewer
What role do you see technology playing in human connection?
Rachel Giannascoli
Technology can either bring us together or push us apart—it depends on how we use it. I'm interested in using digital tools to create deeper, more authentic connections, to break down the barriers that separate us. The goal isn't to replace human interaction, but to enhance it.
