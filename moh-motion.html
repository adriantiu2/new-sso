Interview with Moh motion

Interviewer
Moh, your work often explores the intersection of motion graphics and storytelling. How do you approach creating narratives through movement?
Moh Motion
Motion is language. Every movement, every transition, every gesture tells a story. When I'm creating motion graphics, I'm not just making things move—I'm using movement to communicate emotion, to create rhythm, to guide the viewer's attention and understanding. It's about making the invisible visible.
Interviewer
Your recent project used AI to generate motion graphics based on music. What was the thinking behind this approach?
Moh Motion
I wanted to explore the relationship between sound and movement, between rhythm and visual flow. Music has its own logic, its own emotional arc, and I was curious about how that could translate into visual motion. The AI helped me discover patterns and connections I might not have found on my own.
Interviewer
What's the future of motion graphics? How do you see the field evolving?
Moh Motion
I think we're moving toward motion graphics that are more responsive, more intelligent, more aware of their context. Instead of just playing back predetermined animations, future motion graphics will adapt to their environment, respond to their audience, and create experiences that feel alive and present. It's about making motion feel more human, even when it's created by machines.
